3 Lifestyle Compatibility Questions (Phase 2)

These questions capture lifestyle compatibility without explicit political identification. Similar answers = better match potential.

🏠 Environment Preference

"What type of recovery environment do you prefer?"

☐ No special requirements

☐ Religious dietary laws
🐕 Pet Comfort

"How do you feel about pets in the home?"

☐ Love them, have my own
☐ Comfortable around pets
☐ Prefer no pets (allergies, etc.)
💬 Communication Style

"How often would you like check-ins from your care partner?"

☐ Frequent (every few hours)
☐ Regular (2-3x daily)
☐ As-needed only
⏰ Schedule Preference

"What's your typical daily schedule like?"

☐ Early riser (up by 6am)
☐ Standard (7-8am wake)
☐ Night owl (late mornings)
💡
Why This Approach?

These questions capture genuine lifestyle compatibility that affects day-to-day caregiving success. A morning person paired with a night owl, or someone who needs quiet paired with someone who keeps the TV on all day, can create friction regardless of other factors. This gives us matching signal without asking about sensitive topics directly.

5 Open Questions for Clinical Input

These decisions need clinical expertise to finalize. Current defaults shown - adjust based on experience.

1. Maximum Distance

How far apart can giver and receiver live?

Default: 10 miles
💭 Consider: Denver metro traffic, patient mobility, caregiver availability
2. Sex Matching Preference

Should same-sex matches be strongly preferred, slightly preferred, or neutral?

Default: +3 bonus
💭 Consider: Patient comfort with intimate care tasks, cultural factors
3. Age Bracket Definition

Are current brackets (<65, 65-75, >75) clinically meaningful?

Default: 3 brackets
💭 Consider: Recovery rates, generational compatibility, physical capability
4. Surgery Type Impact

Does hip vs knee vs shoulder affect matching? Different recovery needs?

Default: No factor
💭 Consider: Could same-surgery patients share tips? Or is variety better?
5. Smoking Mismatch Severity

Is -8 penalty appropriate? Should this be a hard blocker instead?

Default: -8 penalty
💭 Consider: Patient complaints about smoke exposure during recovery
6. Giver Capacity

Can one giver support multiple receivers (sequentially)?

Default: 1 at a time
💭 Consider: Giver burnout, commitment level, scheduling complexity
7. Recovery Timeline

How many days between giver's surgery and when they can help someone else?

Default: 14 days
💭 Consider: Varies by surgery type? Patient's own recovery assessment?
